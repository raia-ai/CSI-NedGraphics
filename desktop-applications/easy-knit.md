Edit Toolbar
1. Cut Image - Click to remove the image of the active window and copy it to the clipboard
2. Copy Image - Click to keep the image of the active window and copy it to the clipboard
3. Paste Over Image - Click to paste the contents of the clipboard over the existing image
4. Undo - Click to cancel the last modification made to the design during the current
   session
5. Redo - Click to cancel the effect of the last undo command
6. Undo All - Click to undo a series of actions
7. Reinstate Last But One - Click undo all the way back to the change you wish to make
   then click reinstate and click redo all
8. Redo All - Click to redo all actions altered in Undo

       View Toolbar
       1. Tile Horizontally - Stacks the windows
       2. Tile Vertically - Sets windows side by side
       3. Cascade - Overlaps windows
       4. View Rulers - Keep selected to show rulers
       5. Show Repeat Boundaries - Keep selected to show the repeat boundaries to be shown as
          lines on your monitor.
       6. Tape Measure - To measure the distance between 2 points, click starting point, drag and
          let go at ending point, the distance is recorded on the status bar
       7. Drawing Tool Options - Keep this box open when working with drawing tools for more
          options such as pen size and shape
       8. Show or Hide Palette - Click on to display palette, click off to hide it
       9. Select Colors - Click on button and then click on a color to show it on the palette
       10. Designate Stencil - Click on icon then click on colors in the palette that you want to
           protect
       11. Activate/Disable Stencil - Activates the designated stencil colors, click off to deactivate
           yet still keep them in memory
       12. Zoom Area - To zoom in on a specific area click on icon, then drag box to define the area
           click the mouse button inside to finish
       13. Atlases Manager - View and manage list to linked atlases.
       14. Color Control - This function provides color statistics regarding both the entire image and
            each individual line of the project image
       15. Pattern Draft View - This is the grid view of the design
       16. Simulation - Click to view the knit simulation
       17. Symbol Card - Click to view the symbols that represent the stitches.
       18. Embroidery Visibility - Toggle on/off the visibility of the embroidery layer
       19. Embroidery Layer - Activate the embroidery layer and apply motifs as applique or
            embroidery effects.
       20. Select Embroidery - Click to select several motifs from the embroidery layer
       21. Tracing - Use this icon to trace a file. New Tracing opens dialog box to browse to image
            file.
       22. Move Tracing - Click inside the document and drag to move the tracing behind the design
       23. 3D View - Allows you to view the current knit project mapped onto one of the predefined
            3D images having been supplied with the application                                        3

Drawing Toolbar
*keep drawing tool option menu open for options associated with the Drawing Tools

1. Freehand - Click on this tool and draw lines of various thicknesses and type
2. Clone - Choose an area to reproduce and then choose an area to draw by reproducing
3. Straight Line - Click and drag to draw a straight line of various thickness in any direction or
   horizontal, vertical, or diagonal limitations
4. Draw Circle or Ellipse - Click and drag to draw circles or ellipses either filled or outlined
5. Draw Rectangle or Square - Click and drag to draw squares or rectangles either filled or
   outlined
6. Draw Curve - Click points to draw a curved line of various thicknesses
7. Draw Freehand Shape - Click and drag to draw a filled shape of any kind
8. Fill With Color - Click on a color to fill it with another color
9. Airbrush - Draw using a spray effect of color or cloned area.
10. Smudge - Click and drag across multiple colors to blend those colors together
11. Draw Stripes or Checks - Click and select direction to draw stripes or checks
12. Kaleidoscope - Activates Kaleidoscope tool to create rotated & mirrored designs.
13. Local Outline - Use to outline a shape

Curve Toolbar
1. Freehand Curve- Click this to freehand draw a curve. Double click to stop.
2. Normal Curve- Define the control points of a curve, double-click to stop.
3. Middlepoint Curve- Click on your design to define the start and final control points of a middle
   point curve, double click to finish
4. Edit Control Points- Use to move control points, add and remove control points, and alter
    curves by moving handles.
5. Incrust Curve- Stamps the curve permanently in the design. The curve shape will still be
   active.
6. Remove Curve- Click this button to permanently delete curve.

Motif Toolbar
1. Select Last Used Motif - Activates the last motif you were using
2. Rectangular Selection - Click and drag on design to select a rectangular shaped motif
3. Selection By Color - Click on design to select an area of contiguous color.
4. Lasso Selection - Click and draw to select any shape
5. Local Motif Fill - Fill a color with your active motif
6. Select Whole Image as a Motif - Automatically selects entire design as a motif and places it in
   your Motif Base.
7. Edit Motif - Opens current motif in a new window to edit.
8. Motif Base - Click to opens a Dialog Box containing all the motifs available

Transform Object
1. Flip Object- Click to flip object from top to bottom.
2. Mirror Object- Click to mirror object from side to side.
3. Resize Object- Rescale by clicking and dragging object. If on motif layer, drag corners of
   object.
4. Rotate- Rotate by clicking and dragging object. If on motif layer, drag corners of object.
5. Rotate 90 Degrees- Rotates object 90 degrees clockwise.
6. Rotate 270 Degrees- Rotates object 90 degrees counter clockwise.

Color Tools
1. Exchange colors - Click on first color chip, and then click the second color chip to change the
   position in the palette
2. Swap Colors - Click on first color chip, and then click the second color chip to change the
   position in the design.
3. Merge Colors - Click on first color chip, and then click the second color chip to merge colors
   together by replacing with second color.
4. Copy Color - Click on the color you would like to copy, and then drag and drop it into the color
    you want to replace; both colors will still be in the palette
5. Move Color - Click on the color you want to move, then click on the color that you want it to
   move to.
