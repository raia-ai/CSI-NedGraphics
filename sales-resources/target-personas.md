Target Personas in Apparel Brands for NedGraphics Outreach
1. Print & Textile Designers
Role: Create repeat patterns, colourways, and placement prints.
Need: Efficient tools for pattern creation, colour management, and seamless Adobe integration.
Value to NedGraphics: Direct end-users; strong influence on software selection.
LinkedIn Role Examples:
Textile Designer
Print Designer
Surface Pattern Designer
Graphic Designer (Apparel)
Fashion Print Artist
CAD Textile Designer
What They Do:
Create repeat print patterns, placement graphics, and colourways for fabrics; prepare concepts for seasonal collections; often heavy Adobe Illustrator/Photoshop users.
Why They’re Key for NedGraphics:
They feel the pain of manual repeats and colour separations. Often influence the tools their team adopts if they speed up creative work
2. Design & Product Development Managers
Role: Oversee multiple design streams (wovens, knits, prints) and manage workflow deadlines.
Need: Print suite integration, collaboration features, accurate colour simulation for approvals.
Value to NedGraphics: Decision-makers for software purchasing; focus on productivity and team output.
LinkedIn Role Examples:
Design Manager (Apparel/Textiles)
Head of Design
Senior Product Developer (Textiles)
Creative Director – Fashion/Apparel
Apparel Development Manager,
Category Manager (Textiles/Apparel, i.e., RTW Womenswear, Sportswear, Kids etc.)
What They Do:
Oversee multiple product lines (e.g., wovens, knits, prints), manage design calendars, coordinate between design, technical, and marketing teams.,
Why They’re Key for NedGraphics:,
Make budget decisions for software purchases. Care about speed-to-market, workflow integration, and cost control.
3. Technical & Pre-Production Teams
Role: Prepare design files for manufacturing, including colour separations, repeats, and print specs.
Need: Reliable, production-ready outputs; integration with printers and mills.
Value to NedGraphics: Ensure designs move smoothly from creative to manufacturing without costly rework.
LinkedIn Role Examples:
CAD Technician (Textiles)
Colour Separation Specialist
Textile Technologist
Print Production Coordinator
Pre-Production Manager (Apparel)
Digital Textile Printing Technician
What They Do:
Prepare files for print production (colour separation, repeats, file formatting); ensure colour accuracy; interface with mills, printers, and suppliers.
Why They’re Key for NedGraphics:
Value precision, technical reliability, and integration with printers/RIPs (Raster Image Processor software that translates digital files into raster (bitmap) images that a printer can understand, acting as an interpreter between the computer and the printer). Directly benefit from NedGraphics’ production-ready outputs.
